Visual Auditor for Bias in CNN Image Classifier

What is VisCuit?

Even though CNN image classifiers have shown their superiority in efficiency and accuracy, biases impede the practical applications of the classifiers. However, most existing bias investigation techniques are either inapplicable to general image classification tasks or require a lot of additional efforts to understand. VisCuit is an interactive visualization system that reveals how and why a CNN classifier is biased. VisCuit visually summarizes on which subgroups the classifier underperforms and explains the cause of the low performances by investigating neuron activations while processing the subgroups. Using VisCuit, users can characterize the biases present in the CNN classifier and identify the cause of the biases. As VisCuit is web-based and open-source, users can easily access and extend the tool to other model architectures and datasets.

Who Developed VisCuit?

Led by Seongmin Lee, VisCuit is created by Machine Learning and Human-computer Interaction researchers at Georgia Tech. The team includes Seongmin Lee, Jay Wang, Judy Hoffman, and Polo Chau.
